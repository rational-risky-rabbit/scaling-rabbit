The Scale

How we measure bias.

Inspired by the Political Compass, our scale plots news outlets on two axes instead of a single line. A source can be politically left-leaning yet deeply factual, or politically central yet wildly sensational. One number can't capture that.

Horizontal Axis

Political Lean

From far-left to far-right. Based on framing of economic, social, and policy stories — not just who the outlet endorses.

Vertical Axis

Factual vs Sensational

From dry & factual (top) to emotive & sensational (bottom). Measures charged language, clickbait headlines, and opinion-as-news.

Methodology Note

These positions are editorial judgements based on consistent patterns across many stories, not single events. The scale is a starting point for discussion, not a verdict. Outlets shift over time, and even within a single outlet, different sections (news desk vs opinion vs prime-time) can sit at very different positions on this scale.
